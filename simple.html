The Periods of English Literature

Characteristics of Romanticism

    • Emphasis on emotion and imagination
    • Love for the past, particularly for the middle ages and for the ancient Greek life
    • Addition of strangeness to beauty
    • A subtle sense of mystery
    • An exuberant intellectual curiosity
    • An instinct for the elemental simplicities of life
    • Love for freedom
    • A discard for rules and conventional
    • Love for nature
    • Supernaturalism
    • Subjectivity
    • Prominence of lyricism

The reasons of 1st world war

There are many reasons why the world went to war in 1914.

    • The Naval Race: German Emperor Wilhelm II had a dream of being an imperial ruler. In 1900 he ordered the construction of several large and powerful boats. A race started to have the largest navy. This race led to tension Germany and the British.
    • People across the world were very eager to lead the world how strong and important their country was. Many people thought that their country was better than any other country.
    • A desire for independence: many people in Europe lived in countries that were part of empires. They did not all like being ruled people with different languages and religions.
    • The scramble of Africa — many European nations wanted to have empires. Between 1870 and 1914 much of Africa was colonized by European countries. This led to several arguments between European nations.
    • Conflict between Russia, the Russia-Turkish war.
